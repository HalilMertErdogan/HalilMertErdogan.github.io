Profile

Archaeology built through continuity

Dr. Halil Mert Erdoğan is an archaeologist whose work brings together excavation, architectural analysis, digital documentation, conservation practice and publication.

His research focuses on Lycia, particularly ancient fortifications, Roman religious landscapes, architectural sculpture and the transformation of urban environments.

He has worked at Rhodiapolis, Xanthos, Olympos, Beydağları and Prokonnesos, and currently serves as Deputy Director of the Olympos Excavations.

02 · Zeus Olympios

336 fragments.
One reconstruction.

A large-scale sculptural reconstruction combining archaeological context, architectural evidence, RTI and photogrammetry to identify the cult statue of Zeus Olympios from Olympos.

03 · Hephaistos

From fragmented sculpture to cult identity

The iconographic and archaeological reassessment of a seated male statuette from Olympos, identified as Hephaistos through attributes, context and comparative evidence.

OLBA · 2025

04 · Invictus

Religion, landscape and celestial architecture

Ongoing research on a rock-cut sacred landscape at Olympos, examining the relationship between architecture, solar symbolism, ritual practice and religious syncretism in Roman Lycia.
